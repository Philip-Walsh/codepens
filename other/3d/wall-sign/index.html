💀 The Unseen Footprint of Void 💀

💭 A Potato’s Whisper: Day 322

🌪 The wind that brushed against a puddle last Thursday has now settled somewhere deeper. It rained today, but I barely noticed the drops tapping against the window. The cereal bowl I left on the windowsill is still there—dried flakes clinging to the edges like a stubborn memory. I didn’t clean it, and I don’t think anyone will ever ask why. In a way, it feels like the bowl is teaching me something: that some things are just meant to stay as they are. Some things don’t need closure.

🌻 A Sandwich That Never Was

I made a sandwich. Or rather, I almost did. The bread sat there untouched, waiting for the first slice of tomato. 🍞 A moment of indecision, like the pause before diving into the unknown. I was going to eat it, but then I didn’t. Days passed, and the sandwich never materialized. The ingredients remained untouched, their potential suspended in time. Maybe the sandwich never needed to happen. Maybe it’s all about the idea of it—the anticipation, the pause before the bite.

🪤 Did You Close the Drawer?

The drawer was left open. Or was it closed? I can’t quite remember. What I do know is that the spoon I left inside—my favorite spoon— is still there. It’s a small thing, but it’s also the only thing I can count on. I check it every now and then, just to make sure it’s still in its rightful place. It hasn’t gone anywhere, and neither have I. The drawer, the spoon, this small fragment of my day. It’s as though these small, unremarkable things are the only constants left in a world full of chaos. Maybe the drawer is more than just a drawer. Maybe it’s a place for things to rest before the next big move.

🪑 The desk is empty, but it’s not vacant. There’s something in the air, something unsaid. The chair is pushed aside as though someone was here and left in a hurry. A half-drunk mug sits at the edge—cold now, its warmth long gone. The papers on the desk are all in disarray, as though waiting for a breeze to scatter them into something new. The desk isn’t a place for work anymore; it’s a waiting room for unfinished thoughts. Each item left behind is a whisper of a life lived somewhere in the margins of the day-to-day.

Red is a color, I think. The apple on the counter is red—ripe, yet untouched. 🍏 I’ve walked past it a dozen times. It sits there like a challenge, daring me to pick it up, to bite into it, but I haven’t. Why do we leave things untouched? The apple will wait until it turns soft, and then I’ll wonder why I didn’t eat it sooner. But that’s the funny thing about fruit—it always seems urgent when you first see it, but then it becomes just another object in the room, fading into the background like everything else. Maybe tomorrow.

Rocks are just... rocks. But when piled together, they begin to take on a strange significance. A pile of rocks, unnoticed by most, forms a quiet little monument to the things that don’t seem to matter. Yet, when I look at them, I can’t shake the feeling they’re telling me something. Or maybe they’re just rocks. A pile of forgotten potential, unspoken thoughts, and moments that have passed without ever being acknowledged. But they’re there, like everything else. Waiting.
